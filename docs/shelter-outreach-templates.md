# Shelter Outreach Templates

Use these as starting points. Personalize every message with the shelter's name, recent social media posts, or a specific dog you saw on their site.

---

## Initial Cold Email

**Subject:** Quick question about your adoption process

Hi [Name / Team],

My name is [Your Name], and I'm building a free platform called Wescue that helps connect shelter dogs with adopters based on lifestyle compatibility — things like energy level, living situation, kids, other pets.

I'm not selling anything. I'm reaching out because I'd love to learn about your adoption process and what makes it hard. A 15-minute call or a quick coffee would be incredibly helpful.

A few things about Wescue:
- It's free for shelters. Always will be.
- Only verified rescues and shelters can list. No breeders. No puppy mills.
- We use AI to pre-qualify adopters so you get better-fit applications.

Would you be open to a short conversation? I'm happy to come to you.

Thanks for everything you do for these dogs.

[Your Name]
[Your Phone]
[Your Email]

## Follow-Up (if no response after 5-7 days)

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about your adoption process

Hi [Name / Team],

Just following up on my earlier note. I know you're busy — completely understand if now isn't a good time.

If a quick call doesn't work, I'd also love to just come volunteer for a day and learn how things work behind the scenes.

[Your Name]

## Conversation Starters (for phone or in-person)

Don't pitch. Ask questions and listen.

1. "What does a typical day look like for you?"
2. "Walk me through what happens when someone wants to adopt a dog."
3. "What's the most frustrating part of the adoption process?"
4. "How do you decide if an adopter is a good fit?"
5. "Do you get a lot of applications that don't work out? What goes wrong?"
6. "What tools do you currently use to manage listings and applications?"
7. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about adoption, what would it be?"
8. "Have you tried other platforms like Petfinder or Adopt-a-Pet? What did you think?"
9. "What would make you trust a new platform enough to try it?"
10. "What's the best adoption story you've had recently?"

**The goal of the first conversation is NOT to get them to sign up.** The goal is to understand their world so you can build something they actually want.

## What to Bring to an In-Person Visit

- [ ] A phone or laptop with the Wescue demo loaded
- [ ] A notebook (take physical notes — it shows respect)
- [ ] Business cards if you have them
- [ ] Nothing to sell. Seriously. Just listen.
- [ ] Maybe some donuts or coffee for the staff
